Decoding Hand Signs: An ASL Classification Journey¶

What is ASL? A Journey into a Language of Movement¶

Have you ever spoken with your hands? Not just a wave or thumbs-up, but a whole conversation filled with gestures, expressions, and fluid motion? That's the essence of American Sign Language (ASL).

More than just letters: ASL isn't simply finger-spelling the English alphabet. It's a rich visual language with its own unique grammar, vocabulary, and even facial expressions!

Think in 3D: Imagine your signing space as a canvas. The movement, shape, and placement of your hands all carry meaning. ASL isn't linear, it's multidimensional!

A world of stories, emotions, and ideas: ASL is how a vibrant community connects, shares experiences, and passes down cultural knowledge. It's a language to laugh in, debate in, and even dream in.

Our Quest¶

To build the most proficient American Sign Language (ASL) image classifier, capable of deciphering hand signs with both speed and accuracy. This quest is driven by the potential to unlock powerful tools for communication and learning within the deaf and hard-of-hearing community.

Why it Matters¶

Bridging Communication Gaps: Imagine a world where a real-time ASL translator removes language barriers, fostering seamless interactions and understanding.

Celebrating a Rich Language: By building a technology that 'understands' ASL, we implicitly acknowledge and elevate the beauty and complexity of this visual language.

Weapons of Choice¶

  • InceptionResNetV2 :

    • Why it's suited for the task: With its depth and hybrid Inception modules, InceptionResNetV2 can learn intricate patterns. This is crucial when dealing with a large and nuanced dataset like our ASL collection.
    • Handling Complexity: Think of it like a detective with many specialized tools – each Inception module examines the image from a different angle, allowing the network to make sense of complex handshapes and gestures.

  • Convolutional NN :

    • Finding the Fundamentals: CNNs are masters at identifying basic visual features, like edges and shapes. This forms the foundation for understanding the hand formations essential in ASL.
    • Efficient Backbone: When dealing with big datasets, CNNs offer a relatively computationally efficient way to extract those vital building blocks for sign recognition.

Insight

The dataset exhibits a significant class imbalance, with certain ASL signs appearing much more frequently than others. For example, the sign for the letter 'o' is overrepresented with 7294 samples compared to signs like 'w' or 'z' having far fewer.

This imbalance can cause machine learning models to become biased towards the overrepresented classes. If not addressed, the model may perform well on frequent signs but struggle to accurately recognize less common ones.

  1. Image Data Generator

We've set up image generators to streamline how our ASL classifier will handle the image data. First, we've included a preprocessing step specifically tailored for the InceptionResNetV2 model we're using. Next, we've added some clever data augmentation – zooming, rotating, and shifting images slightly during training. This helps make our dataset seem larger and prevents the model from getting too comfortable with any specific image orientation. Finally, the generators will neatly pull the images and their corresponding labels from our dataframes, making sure our model is fed a steady stream of data during training, validation, and testing.

def plot_accuracy_loss(history):
    fig, (ax1, ax2) = plt.subplots(nrows=1, ncols=2, figsize=(10, 4)) 
    fig.suptitle("Accuracy & Loss")
    ax1.plot(history['accuracy'], color='teal', label='accuracy')
    ax1.plot(history['val_accuracy'], color='orange', label='val_accuracy')
    ax1.set_title('Accuracy', fontsize=20)
    ax1.set_xlabel("Epochs")
    ax1.legend(loc="lower right")
    
    ax2.plot(history['loss'], color='teal', label='loss')
    ax2.plot(history['val_loss'], color='orange', label='val_loss')
    ax2.set_title('Loss', fontsize=20)
    ax2.set_xlabel("Epochs")
    ax2.legend(loc="upper right")
    fig.tight_layout()
    plt.show()

def build_cnn():
    model = Sequential()
    model.add(Conv2D(75, (3, 3), strides=1, padding='same', activation='relu', input_shape=(IMAGE_SIZE, IMAGE_SIZE, 3)))
    model.add(tf.keras.layers.BatchNormalization())
    model.add(MaxPooling2D((2, 2), strides=2, padding='same'))
    model.add(Conv2D(50, (3, 3), strides=1, padding='same', activation='relu'))
    model.add(tf.keras.layers.Dropout(0.2))
    model.add(tf.keras.layers.BatchNormalization())
    model.add(MaxPooling2D((2, 2), strides=2, padding='same'))
    model.add(Conv2D(25, (3, 3), strides=1, padding='same', activation='relu'))
    model.add(tf.keras.layers.BatchNormalization())
    model.add(MaxPooling2D((2, 2), strides=2, padding='same'))
    model.add(Flatten())
    model.add(Dense(units=512, activation='relu'))
    model.add(tf.keras.layers.Dropout(0.3))
    model.add(Dense(units=36, activation='softmax'))

    model.compile(optimizer='SGD', loss='categorical_crossentropy', metrics=['accuracy'])
    model.summary()
    return model
